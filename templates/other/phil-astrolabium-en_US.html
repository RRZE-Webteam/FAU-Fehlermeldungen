One such story is told by the Arabian scholar Ibn Khallikan, telling how the astrolabe was invented after what at first seemed a stroke of bad luck.

The astrolabe is a versatile instrument used by astronomers, astrologers and mathematicians to make astronomical measurements and other calculations until well into the modern period. It looks like a two-dimensional model of the three-dimensional celestial sphere and it was invented as follows, so the story goes: Ptolemy, a famous astrologer and astronomer who lived in Alexandria in the 2nd century, suffered a mishap. Whilst riding on a donkey, he dropped an armillary sphere, a three dimensional model of the globe. According to legend, the donkey trod on the sphere, making a two dimensional version of the original three dimensional globe. The astrolabe was invented.
